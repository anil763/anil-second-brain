## 1. I Was That Suicidal Kid

**Duration:** ~2-2.5 min | **Platform:** TikTok/Instagram/YouTube | **Goal:** Raw vulnerability + Hope

### HOOK (0-10 sec)

"I almost didn't make it past 12. Here's what saved me."

*(Pause. Direct eye contact. Let that land.)*

### BODY (10s - 2:00)

"I was a suicidal child. That's not easy to say, but it's true.

I remember lying in bed thinking — *why am I here? Nobody wants me. Nobody would even notice if I was gone.*

I was carrying pain that no kid should carry. Pain I didn't have words for. Pain that felt like it would swallow me whole.

And for years — *decades* — I thought that pain was my curse. My brokenness. Something shameful to hide.

But here's what I've learned on the other side of that darkness:

**That pain wasn't a mistake. It was my training ground.**

Every wound I survived? It taught me how to help others survive theirs.

That little kid who wanted to disappear? He's the reason I'm standing here now, talking to you.

I'm not sharing this for sympathy. I'm sharing this because if you're in that dark place right now — or you've been there — I need you to hear something:

**Healing is possible.**

Not in some perfect, shiny, Instagram way. But in a real, messy, *holy shit I can't believe I made it* kind of way.

Your pain has purpose. Even if you can't see it yet. Especially if you can't see it yet.

I was meant to go through that. So I could be here. For you. Right now."

### TAKEAWAY ACTION (2:00 - end)

"Here's what I want you to do today — just one thing:

**One small act of self-love.**

That's it. Maybe it's five minutes alone with your coffee. Maybe it's not saying yes when you want to say no. Maybe it's just looking in the mirror and saying, *'I see you. You're still here. That matters.'*

Start there. Start with one."

### CTA

"If this hit you somewhere — if some part of you needed to hear this — drop a ❤️ or leave a comment. I read every one.

And if you want more real talk about healing without the woo-woo BS, hit follow. I'm here every day."

### PRODUCTION NOTES

**Tone:** Intimate, serious but warm. Not dramatic — grounded. This is a conversation, not a performance.

**Setting:** Simple. Close-up. Neutral background. Nothing distracting. The words carry this.

**Visuals:** Direct to camera. Minimal cuts. Let silence breathe after the hook.

**Pacing:** Slow opening. Pick up slightly in the middle teaching section. Slow again for takeaway.

**Warning:** Consider adding crisis line info in description/pinned comment.

## 2. The Day I Chose Myself

**Duration:** ~2-2.5 min | **Platform:** TikTok/Instagram/YouTube | **Goal:** Permission + Liberation

### HOOK (0-10 sec)

"40 years of choosing everyone else. Then I made ONE decision that changed everything."

*(Beat. Small head shake. Almost disbelief.)*

### BODY (10s - 2:00)

"I was the guy who said yes to everything.

Need me to work late? Sure. Want me to drop my plans? No problem. My needs? They can wait. They always wait.

I thought I was being a good person. A good employee. A good partner. A good son.

But you know what I actually was?

**Gone.**

I had abandoned myself so completely that I didn't even know what I wanted anymore. Someone could ask me, 'What do you need?' and I'd blank. I had no idea.

Then one day — middle of a regular Tuesday — I was about to say yes to something I didn't want to do. Again. And something in me just... broke open.

Not dramatically. Quietly. Like a whisper that said:

*'What about you?'*

And I made a choice. I said no.

That's it. I just said no.

But that tiny 'no' cracked everything open.

Because here's the truth nobody tells you:

**You're allowed to choose yourself.**

Not in a selfish, screw-everybody way. In a *'I matter too'* way. In an *'I can't pour from an empty cup'* way.

Choosing yourself isn't abandoning others. It's finally including yourself in the people you care for.

For 40 years, I left myself off that list. 

One word changed my life. No."

### TAKEAWAY ACTION (2:00 - end)

"Here's your assignment — and yeah, it's uncomfortable:

**Identify ONE thing you're doing for someone else that you don't actually want to do.**

Just one. You don't have to stop doing it yet. Just notice it. Name it.

Because awareness is the first step. Once you see the pattern, you can't unsee it.

What's your one thing?"

### CTA

"If you felt that — if something in you said *'finally, someone gets it'* — like this and follow.

I'm sharing what 40 years of self-abandonment taught me. So maybe you don't have to wait that long."

### PRODUCTION NOTES

**Tone:** Conversational, slightly wry. GenX energy — we can laugh at ourselves a little even when it's real.

**Setting:** Casual. Could be sitting, could be standing. Natural light preferred.

**Visuals:** More animated than Video 1. Some hand gestures. This one has energy.

**Pacing:** Build through the story, land hard on "You're allowed to choose yourself," then soften for takeaway.

## 3. Your Heart Knows What Your Mind Won't Admit

**Duration:** ~2-2.5 min | **Platform:** TikTok/Instagram/YouTube | **Goal:** Teaching + Intuition Awakening

### HOOK (0-10 sec)

"Your intuition has been trying to tell you something. Are you listening? ...Or are you arguing with it?"

*(Knowing look. Slight smile.)*

### BODY (10s - 2:00)

"I used to live in my head. Like, *live* there. Rent-free tenant in my own brain.

Every decision went through seventeen layers of analysis. Pro/con lists. What-if scenarios. Asking everyone's opinion but my own.

And you know what? I made terrible decisions. All the time.

Because my mind is a professional overthinker. It can rationalize anything. It can logic me right into situations that destroy me.

But my heart? My heart always knew.

That job that looked great on paper but made me sick every Sunday night? My heart knew.

That relationship everyone approved of but felt wrong? My heart knew.

That dream I kept dismissing as 'unrealistic'? My heart knew.

**Your heart knows what your mind won't admit.**

The mind deals in logic. The heart deals in truth.

And here's the wild thing — when I finally started listening to my heart, life got simpler. Not easier. Simpler.

Because the answers were already there. I just had to stop arguing with them.

Now I run everything through my heart first. Not just the big stuff — everything.

Does this feel expansive or contractive? Does this feel like truth or like should?

Your heart is your GPS. Your mind is the passenger who won't stop giving directions.

Maybe it's time to let your heart drive."

### TAKEAWAY ACTION (2:00 - end)

"Here's what I want you to try today:

**Sit with your heart for 5 minutes.**

Not meditation — unless you want to. Just... check in.

Put your hand on your chest. Take a breath. And ask:

*'What do you want me to know?'*

Then just... listen. Whatever comes up — a word, a feeling, an image, nothing — that's okay.

You're rebuilding a connection that's been ignored. It takes time.

5 minutes. Today. That's all."

### CTA

"Drop a comment and tell me — what has your intuition been trying to tell you that you keep ignoring?

I'll go first: For years, mine was saying 'slow down.' I didn't listen. I paid for it.

Follow for more heart-led wisdom — no guru BS, just real talk."

### PRODUCTION NOTES

**Tone:** Warm, slightly playful at the start, deepening through the middle. Teacher energy without being preachy.

**Setting:** Somewhere that feels calm. Maybe natural elements visible. This one benefits from a softer environment.

**Visuals:** Hand on heart during the takeaway section. Genuine, not performative.

**Pacing:** Conversational rhythm. Let the "Your heart knows what your mind won't admit" line breathe — it's the anchor.

## 4. I Was Meant for This

**Duration:** ~2-2.5 min | **Platform:** TikTok/Instagram/YouTube | **Goal:** Purpose + Reframe

### HOOK (0-10 sec)

"The thing that almost killed me? It's why I'm here now."

*(Pause. Let them wonder what you mean.)*

### BODY (10s - 2:00)

"For years, I asked the wrong question.

*'Why did this happen to me?'*

Why the trauma? Why the pain? Why did I have to struggle so hard just to survive?

It felt random. Cruel. Like the universe had it out for me specifically.

But then the question flipped:

*'What if this happened FOR me?'*

And I know — I KNOW — that sounds like toxic positivity. Stay with me.

I'm not saying trauma is good. I'm not saying pain is a gift. That's bullshit.

What I'm saying is: **the wound can become the wisdom.**

Everything I went through? It taught me things I couldn't have learned any other way.

Empathy you can't fake. Resilience you can't buy. An understanding of darkness that lets me sit with others in theirs without flinching.

I was meant to go through this. Not because I deserved to suffer. But because somewhere, there's someone who needs exactly what I learned in that fire.

**Your pain is your purpose.**

Not because suffering is noble. But because you survived something. And that survival story? It's medicine for someone else.

You didn't just go through it. You went through it so you could come back and light the path for others.

That's not a wound. That's a calling."

### TAKEAWAY ACTION (2:00 - end)

"Here's your reflection for today:

**What has your struggle taught you?**

Not the polished, inspirational poster version. The real version.

What do you know now — about yourself, about life, about people — that you only know because you went through that hard thing?

Write it down. Just for you. Because that's the seed of your purpose.

What you survived is what you're meant to share."

### CTA

"If you've ever felt like your pain was pointless — and then started to see the purpose — I want to hear your story.

Drop it in the comments. Let's build something here.

And follow for more content about turning wounds into wisdom."

### PRODUCTION NOTES

**Tone:** Serious but hopeful. Not dramatic — earned. This requires gravitas.

**Setting:** Simple, grounded. This message doesn't need visual bells and whistles.

**Visuals:** Steady eye contact. Minimal movement. Let the words land.

**Pacing:** Deliberate. The question flip ("for me" instead of "to me") needs space. Don't rush the reframe.

**Note:** Be careful not to minimize real trauma. The teaching is about meaning-making, not toxic positivity.

## 5. Safe to Be Loved

**Duration:** ~2-2.5 min | **Platform:** TikTok/Instagram/YouTube | **Goal:** Deep healing + Core wound work

### HOOK (0-10 sec)

"For 40 years I didn't believe I was safe to be loved. Now I do. And it changed my entire life."

*(Vulnerability visible. This is tender.)*

### BODY (10s - 2:00)

"I'm going to tell you the most important realization of my life.

For decades, I didn't believe I deserved love.

Oh, I WANTED it. Desperately. But deep down, in that place where our oldest stories live, I believed:

*If people really knew me — the real me — they wouldn't love me. They couldn't.*

So I performed. I people-pleased. I hid the messy parts. I armored up and called it independence.

And you know what happened?

I got love that never felt real. Because they weren't loving the real me. They were loving the mask.

Then one day, in the middle of doing deep inner work, something shifted.

I was sitting with all my shame, all my mess, all my wounds... and this thought dropped in like a bomb:

**'I am safe to be loved.'**

Not 'I deserve love' — that felt too far. 

'I am SAFE to be loved.'

Safe to let someone in. Safe to be seen. Safe to receive.

And I broke. In the best way.

Because when you believe you're safe to be loved, you stop performing. You stop hiding. You stop settling for crumbs.

You let love actually IN.

Love is your birthright. You don't have to earn it. You don't have to be perfect for it. You are safe to receive it.

That scared little kid inside you who decided they were unlovable? 

They were wrong. They were protecting themselves. But they were wrong."

### TAKEAWAY ACTION (2:00 - end)

"Here's something simple that might not feel simple:

**Name one person who loves you.**

Not who you THINK loves you. Who actually, genuinely, loves you.

Say their name. Out loud if you can.

Feel that. Someone loves you RIGHT NOW. Exactly as you are.

You are safe to receive that.

Let yourself receive it."

### CTA

"This one hit different for me to share. If it landed for you, let me know.

Comment the name of one person who loves you. Let's flood this comment section with evidence that we are, in fact, safe to be loved.

Follow for more real talk about healing. No perfection. Just truth."

### PRODUCTION NOTES

**Tone:** Tender, intimate, vulnerable. This is the softest of the five. Let the emotion be visible.

**Setting:** Quiet, simple. Close-up. This is a heart-to-heart conversation.

**Visuals:** Soft eye contact. It's okay if this one gets emotional — authentic emotion lands.

**Pacing:** Slow throughout. This isn't rushed. Let "I am safe to be loved" sit in silence for a beat.

**Note:** This is core wound work. Some viewers may have strong emotional responses. Consider supportive resources in description.

## Series Notes

### Posting Order

Can be posted in any order, but this sequence builds trust:
1. "I Was That Suicidal Kid" — establishes vulnerability and credibility
2. "Safe to Be Loved" — goes deep into core wounds
3. "The Day I Chose Myself" — empowerment and action
4. "Your Heart Knows" — practical spiritual teaching
5. "I Was Meant for This" — meaning-making and purpose

### Batch Recording Tips

- Film all 5 in one session while in the zone
- Different shirts for variety
- Same location keeps branding consistent
- Film in natural light (morning or golden hour)
- Phone is fine — authenticity > production value
